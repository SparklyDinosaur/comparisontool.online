About this tool

Background and methodology for the BMA Medical Schools Comparison Tool.

Purpose

The UK Medical Schools Comparison Tool was developed by the BMA Medical Students Committee (MSC) to provide a structured, data-driven overview of support structures across UK medical schools.

The tool is intended to help prospective and current medical students compare institutions on a number of practical factors that may affect their experience of studying medicine in the UK.

What is compared

The tool covers the following categories of data:

  • International tuition fees — reported annual fees for international students across pre-clinical and clinical years
  • Placement travel support — the systems used for reimbursing or funding travel to clinical placements
  • Welfare and leave provisions — attendance requirements, flexibility provisions, and leave policies
  • Resource provision — clinical equipment and learning resources provided to students
  • Financial support — hardship funds, research grants, and elective-related funding

Data and accuracy

Data has been compiled from publicly available sources and direct correspondence with medical schools. It is provided for reference purposes only.

Figures and data points may be subject to change. Users are encouraged to verify all information directly with the relevant institution before making decisions based on this tool.

This tool does not rank or assess medical schools. Data is presented for comparison purposes only.

Contact and updates

To request updates to data or to report inaccuracies, please contact the BMA Medical Students Committee.
